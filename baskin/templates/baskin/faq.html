Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly is solar energy?

Solar energy is defined as the energy obtained from the sun and turned into thermal or electrical energy. Solar energy is a clean, renewable source of energy. When sunlight strikes a photovoltaic cell, it creates electricity via an electrical process and stores it in batteries via a chemical process.

Solar energy may be utilised to prepare meals using solar cookers. With the help of a solar still, it may also be utilised to transform salty water into drinking water. Finally, solar energy may be turned into electrical energy and used to power a variety of devices using a photovoltaic cell.

How Do Solar Panels on Your House Work?

Solar panels on your home generate direct current by converting photons from the sun. This current is then fed into your inverter. Subsequently, your inverter converts the direct current to alternating current and transmits it to your electric box to power your house.

What exactly is net metering?

If your solar energy system is grid-connected to your electric provider and generates more electricity than the property needs at any one time, the excess power will go to the electric provider. Over the course of a month, if a solar energy system transmits more electricity into the grid than the residence consumes, the difference, or net, will result in a credit decided by the electric provider. Net metering "credits" might be as low as the utility's base rate for wholesale electricity or as high as the retail rate you pay as a customer. Net metering is not yet available from all power companies. Contact your energy provider (or providers if you reside in a deregulated region).
